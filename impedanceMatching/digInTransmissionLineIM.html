Design of a transmission-line impedance matching circuits

Transmission-line impedance matching circuits are used at higher frequencies where the lumped elements become very small and impractical to use.

To design fully transmission-line matching circuits, we have to first learn how to replace the lumped element in the matching circuit from the last step in the previous section with a transmission line.

Replacing lumped-elements with equivalent transmission-line stubs

To make fully transmission line impedance matching circuits, we can replace capacitors and inductors with “stubs”, which are shorted or open transmission lines.

The input impedance of shorted or open transmission lines can be made purely inductive or capacitive, as shown in Figures fig:OpenStubLambdaOver8-fig:ShortedStubLambdaOver8. SWR circle of an open or shorted stub is the outer perimeter of the Smith Chart. The length of the transmission line will determine the input impedance of the stub. The input impedance is always purely reactive.
