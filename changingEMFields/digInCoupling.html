Coils with coupling

When two coils are close together, their magnetic fields couple and some of the flux from coil 1 will pass through the second coil. This additional flux produces induced voltage in the second coil, as we discussed before. The amount of interaction between the coils is called mutual flux. Associated with mutual flux is mutual inductance . Mutual inductance can be positive or negative, as we have seen before because the fluxes can add or subtract. The mutual flux through coil 1 can be in phase or out of phase with the current that produced it.
